Concept

Adjust angle (0-360°, default golden angle). Adjust seed count (50-2000). Toggle color modes: by index, by spiral arm, rainbow. Animate the angle to see patterns form and break. Size scaling options.

The Math

Position of seed n: r = c·√n, θ = n·137.507...°. The golden angle = 360°·(1 - 1/φ) where φ = (1+√5)/2. Fibonacci numbers appear in the spiral arm counts (8, 13, 21, 34...).

String Art GEOMETRIC

Connect numbered pins with strings following a rule — cardioids, nephriods, and stunning geometric envelopes emerge from straight lines.

How it works
Concept

Place pins around a circle. Connect pin n to pin (n×k) mod total. Different multipliers k create different curves: k=2 → cardioid, k=3 → nephroid, k=4 → 3-cusped epicycloid. Straight lines create curved envelopes.

Controls

Adjust number of pins (20-360), multiplier k (2-50), and line opacity. Animate k to morph between patterns. Color modes: solid, rainbow by connection, gradient by angle. Pin shape: circle, polygon, custom.

The Math

Connect pin i to pin (i×k) mod N. The envelope of these chords forms a curve called an epicycloid. When k and N share factors, fewer distinct strings appear. Prime N gives the most complex patterns.

Pixel Sorter GLITCH ART

Sort pixels by brightness, hue, or saturation — creating stunning glitch art effects. The intersection of algorithms and aesthetics.

How it works
Concept

Take an image (or generated pattern), find spans of pixels above/below a brightness threshold, and sort those spans. The result is a distinctive "dripping" glitch aesthetic that's become iconic in digital art.

Controls

Choose source: gradient, noise, circles, photo upload. Sort mode: brightness, hue, saturation. Direction: horizontal, vertical, both. Threshold slider controls which pixels get sorted. Animate for continuous glitch effect.

The Math

For each row/column: find contiguous spans where brightness > threshold. Sort pixels within each span by chosen criterion. Threshold creates the boundary between sorted (glitched) and unsorted (preserved) regions.

Ray Caster 2.5D

Walk through a maze in first-person 2.5D — the same rendering technique behind Wolfenstein 3D. Cast rays, draw walls, feel the depth.

How it works
Concept

For each column of pixels, cast a ray from the player into the map. When it hits a wall, calculate the distance. Closer walls are drawn taller, creating a convincing 3D illusion from a 2D map — the technique that launched the FPS genre.

Controls

WASD or arrow keys to move and turn. Mouse to look around. Split view: minimap (top-down with rays) + 3D view. Toggle floor/ceiling rendering, fog distance, wall textures. Generate random mazes.

The Math

DDA (Digital Differential Analyzer) ray stepping through grid. Wall height = canvas_height / perpendicular_distance (fishbowl correction). Texture mapping via wall hit position. Floor casting via scanline.
